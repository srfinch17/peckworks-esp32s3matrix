1 · Per-channel ON/OFF floors

For each channel: click Show ramp (a gradient of that channel, dim → bright across the 64 cells, full brightness). Find the first cell that lights (count left→right, top→bottom, 1–64) and enter it — that cell's value is the channel's floor. Use Show sweep + the slider as a cross-check (dim a full-channel field until it goes dark).

2 · White balance

Click Show patches — three equal-value bands (R | G | B). They probably look unequal (green usually dominates). Lower the brighter bands' values until all three look equally bright, then Compute gains. The dimmest channel ends at gain 1.0; brighter channels get attenuated.

3 · Gamma (fade ramp)

Click Show ramp — an 8-row grey ramp on the board. Do the dark steps bunch together? Drag the slider until the on-screen swatches below match the panel's perceived spacing; that's the gamma.

γ 1.00

4 · Secondary & mixed colors

For each: Show fills the panel with that color. Does it read as the named hue at full brightness? At low brightness (drop the panel brightness)? Nudge the picker until it reads true, then Record. Brightness here uses /api/brightness (persists to the board); Save leaves it at this slider's value.

5 · Per-pixel uniformity (optional)

Walk one white pixel across all 64 to spot any that are dimmer or hue-shifted. Note outlier indices (0–63, comma-separated). Leave blank if the panel looks uniform — most boards do, and we ship no per-pixel trim then.
